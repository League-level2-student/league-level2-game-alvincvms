Pixel Legend

by: Alvin (Aiping) Wang

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I originally have the ideas of making an RPG games or strategy games as these are all types of games I loved to play, but I chose to make this Plat former-like game because the fact that I've recently been playing a game called "Dead Cells". It is a great action game that combined platformer, rouguelike, and castlevania styles of game together, and is why I got the inspiration for making this game.

2. What I Learned

I added different mechanics such as damage reduction at different stages in the boss fight and different damages for melee and ranged attacks from feedbacks of classmates after they've tested the game. My teacher had also guided me at the part about movements of the character.

3. Architecture of My Game

My game is a platformer 2D-fighting game that consists of fighting enemies, beating levels, and defeat the final boss. The game consisted of multiple classes, including one Runner class (named "PixelLegend"); one Game class that includes the timer, controls different states of the game, and takes inputs; one Manager class to control the spawning of monsters, damage received and dealt; one Player class that controls the player's character; one Object class that builds the structure of all objects; and fellow game object classes that uses the Object or the Monster class.

4. Technical Implementation

I'm proud of the fighting part of the game and the balanced health and damage the enemies has. It took multiple rounds of testing to make sure the enemies' strength in different difficulties are perfectly balanced, like all things should be.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part is creating the platforms' physics in the game. My teacher helped me and I used online examples to overcome the issue.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I could add more levels, types of enemies, new bosses, weapon & items system, new sound effects, backgrounds, interactive objects, etc.
